<Intro>
In the early 2010s, audiences were increasingly engaging with television content online, discovering new programs, following their favorite shows, and interacting with entertainment beyond the television screen.

As the UK's largest commercial broadcaster, Sky wanted to create a single home for Sky TV — a destination where viewers could discover content, engage with the shows and movies they cared about, and access a growing range of digital experiences. In this evolving landscape, I led the redesign of Sky's content discovery experience, creating a shared framework that brought greater consistency across Sky's digital properties while supporting new approaches to content discovery and engagement.

The result was a more coherent and scalable foundation for Sky's online content experiences and an important step in the company's broader effort to build audience engagement beyond the broadcast schedule.
</Intro>

---

At a time when audiences were beginning to spend more time engaging with television content online, Sky's digital experiences were becoming an increasingly important part of how viewers interacted with the brand.

The challenge was that these experiences lacked a common structure. Individual channel sites often used different information architectures, navigation models, and content strategies. Flagship programs frequently had dedicated microsites created by external agencies, each with different structures, interaction patterns, and visual approaches.

While viewers experienced Sky as a single brand, the digital experience often felt very different depending on which channel they were exploring.

One of the project's primary goals was to create a consistent experience for viewers while supporting the diverse range of content and experiences that existed across Sky's digital properties.

Working closely with engineering and visual design, I developed a common information architecture and a set of shared design patterns that could be applied across channel sites and content experiences. This created a more consistent experience for viewers while making it easier for teams to launch and manage new content destinations.

Rather than treating each site as an isolated destination, the framework established a connected platform where viewers could move easily between channels, programs, movies, and related content.

Some of the most complex requirements emerged around Sky Movies.

Unlike the TV channel sites, the Movies experience needed to support subscription content, pay-per-view releases, curated collections, reviews, and promotional content within a single experience. The framework needed to accommodate these different models without requiring a completely separate design language, allowing the Movies experience to maintain its unique needs while still feeling part of the broader Sky platform.

Alongside the platform work, we explored new ways to help viewers discover content.

One of the challenges facing broadcasters at the time was helping audiences become aware of a large library of content they might not otherwise discover. Traditional search experiences typically relied on structured forms that asked viewers to filter by title, genre, actors, and other predefined criteria.

We explored a number of alternative approaches, including personalized taste profiles, recommendation systems, and natural-language search concepts that would allow viewers to describe what they wanted to watch in everyday language rather than forcing them to navigate complex search interfaces.

<Media src="/case-04-content-discovery-movies.png" caption="We explored natural-language search at a time when structured filters and forms were still the standard way to discover content.." />

These explorations were supported by user research and helped the team think beyond traditional navigation models at a time when many of the conventions that define modern entertainment platforms today were only beginning to emerge.

The project created a shared foundation for Sky's online content experiences, bringing greater consistency across channel sites while making it easier for teams to launch and evolve new content destinations.

<Media src="/case-04-content-discovery-responsive.jpg" caption="The platform created a single home for Sky TV, connecting channels, programs, and premium features across devices." />

Looking back, many of the ideas we were exploring around discovery, personalization, and audience engagement have since become commonplace across entertainment platforms. At the time, however, they represented some of the earliest attempts to rethink how audiences could engage with television content beyond the broadcast schedule.
